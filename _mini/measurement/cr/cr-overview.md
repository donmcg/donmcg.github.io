# Compression Ratio

**Compression ratio (CR)** is the ratio between the maximum
*(Bottom Dead Center)* and minimum *(Top Dead Center)* volume during the
compression stage of the power cycle in a piston engine.


Rebuilding an engine, by its very nature, results in a bespoke product
which no longer conforms to original factory specifications.
The standard operations of boring or decking a block, fitting new pistons,
and skimming the head all lead to changes in the compression ratio.

The compression ratio strongly influences the power developed by
the engine.  However it also strongly influences the grade of fuel
required for that engine to operate reliably.  Higher CRs develop
more power but require higher octane gasoline.

The misadventure leading to these pages was the result of rebuilding
an 1275 A+ engine intended for modest road use. By simply reworking
the block and head, with no goal to boost performance, the resulting
CR weighed in at 11.3, far above the factory value of 9.8.
In this state the engine suffered from pinking and wildly mismatched
carburetion.
